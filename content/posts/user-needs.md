When you want to look for user pain points, go to a bar in the middle of the week, look for the most desparate looking person and buy them a beer. They'd probably have something substantial to talk about. While I don't usually go to bars during the week, I've found some similar ways of identifying user needs, mostly through conversations or online forums. This is a running document of the user needs or pain points that I've found to be interesting. Perhaps some of them will turn out to be viable business oppportunities. 

## The mundane and apparent ones:
- Career seeking: it's a difficult job market, whether it's doing leetcode or preparing for behavioral interviews, people want a way to cheat their way out of this difficult process, and they're willing to pay for it too.
