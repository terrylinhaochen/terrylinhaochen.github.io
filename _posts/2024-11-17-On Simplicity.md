Every so often, there is a product that makes a ripple. Be there when it happens. 

The challenge lies in the nature of truly noteworthy products – they rarely burst onto the scene overnight. Instead, they often represent years of iteration, refinement, and growth. While tracking promising startups is one skill, identifying fundamental innovations that could lead to billion-dollar market opportunities requires a deeper understanding that cuts through the noise. 

Though I'm still trying to develop my own worldview, I've noticed a interesting pattern among exceptional founders: they embrace simplicity. These leaders have an uncanny ability to call out BS and explain terms easily understandable by a 10-year-old. I've also seen highly reputable people concepualized and absract things, though it would sound somewhat profund first time you've heard it, when you actually think about it, there is no way of simplifying it into basic human needs (these people did not create note worthy products, yet they've risen to the top ranks of managers).

Put simply, keep it simple, don't BS.
