I never listen to recordings of myself talking. Yet I love having conversations: while walking, at coffee shops, and even on podcasts. We are all no stranger to the conversation format and can easily be producing content, embodiments of our thoughts and ideas through the spoken language. I think we are at a moment where the way we interface with spoken language is about to change. Think of having a graphics user interface for conversations. How would that look? There wouldn't be a physical embodiment, yet everything that makes conversations so natural can now be part of the podcast (or narrated video) experience. "What you said about Peet's", references and grounding can now be conveyed in full. Instead of section headings, all the information can be conveyed via the appropriate format when you need it. NotebookLM is an interesting concept, yet is there an opportunity for a fundamentally new content format?
