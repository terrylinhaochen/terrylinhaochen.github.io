Every so often, a product comes along that makes a ripple. Be there when it happens. However, the challenge of identifying these startups lies in the nature of truly noteworthy products – they rarely burst onto the scene overnight. Instead, they often represent years of iteration, refinement, and growth. While tracking promising startups is one skill, identifying fundamental innovations that could lead to billion-dollar market opportunities requires a deeper understanding that cuts through the noise.

Interestingly, I've noticed that truly exceptional founders all embrace simplicity. These leaders have an uncanny ability to call out BS and explain complex terms in ways a child could understand. I've also seen highly reputable people conceptualize and abstract things in ways that sound profound at first hearing. However, when you actually think about their ideas, they often can't be simplified into basic human needs. These people, while rising to top management positions, rarely create noteworthy products.
